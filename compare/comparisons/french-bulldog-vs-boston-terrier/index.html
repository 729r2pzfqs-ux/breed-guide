French Bulldog vs Boston Terrier

Two adorable compact companions perfect for city living. Both are charming and adaptable—but they have distinct personalities!

French Bulldog

The Trendy City Dog

The French Bulldog has enjoyed a massive surge in popularity. The adaptable Frenchie is the ideal companion for city dwellers—they don't need a lot of space and are content with short walks.

French Bulldog Temperament

Frenchies are charming, adaptable, and make excellent companions. They're playful but not overly energetic. They love human contact and get along well with other pets.

Boston Terrier Temperament

Boston Terriers are excellent with children and make wonderful family pets. Their intelligence and eagerness to please make training enjoyable. They're known for their comical expressions!

Important Health Considerations

Both breeds are brachycephalic (flat-faced), which means they share some common health concerns:

French Bulldog:
  • • Breathing difficulties (BOAS)
  • • Overheating issues
  • • Spinal disorders
  • • Skin fold infections
  • • Higher vet costs expected
Boston Terrier:
  • • Breathing issues (less severe)
  • • Eye problems (prominent eyes)
  • • Patellar luxation
  • • Deafness (in some)
  • • Generally fewer health issues

Which Is Right For You?

Choose a French Bulldog If...

  • You want a very low-energy companion
  • You prefer a stockier, more muscular build
  • You love the bat ear look
  • You want more color variety
  • You can budget for higher vet costs

Choose a Boston Terrier If...

  • You want a slightly more active companion
  • You prefer a leaner, taller build
  • You love the "tuxedo" markings
  • You have children (slightly better)
  • You want generally fewer health issues

The Bottom Line

Both breeds are perfect for apartment living and first-time owners. Frenchies are lower energy and more expensive, while Bostons are slightly more active with fewer health concerns. Either will bring endless charm to your life!
