Siberian Husky vs Alaskan Malamute

Two magnificent Arctic breeds often confused for each other! Both are stunning, powerful, and challenging—but they have key differences you should know.

❄️

Arctic Working Dogs

Both breeds were developed to work in extreme cold. They thrive in cold climates and may struggle in hot weather. If you live somewhere warm, reconsider these breeds!

Siberian Husky

The Swift Runner

Siberian Huskies are beautiful, athletic dogs bred to run. They're friendly, outgoing, and mischievous. Known for their striking blue eyes and wolf-like appearance.

View full profile →

Alaskan Malamute

The Powerful Hauler

The Alaskan Malamute is one of the oldest Arctic sled dogs, originally bred for hauling heavy freight. They're powerful, substantial dogs with a friendly disposition.

🔍 Quick Visual ID

Siberian Husky

  • • Medium size (16-27 kg)
  • • Can have blue, brown, or mixed eyes
  • • Ears closer together, pointed
  • • Sleeker, more athletic build
  • • Tail usually down or curved

Alaskan Malamute

  • • Large size (34-39 kg)
  • • Always brown eyes (never blue)
  • • Ears wider apart, more rounded
  • • Heavier, more muscular build
  • • Tail curls over back (plume)

🧠 Husky Temperament

Outgoing Mischievous Loyal Friendly Gentle Alert

Huskies are outgoing, mischievous, and love everyone—they make terrible guard dogs! They're escape artists and need secure fencing. Very "talkative" with distinctive howling.

🧠 Malamute Temperament

Loyal Playful Affectionate Independent Dignified Friendly

Malamutes are friendly with people but can be aggressive toward other dogs, especially same-sex dogs. More dignified than Huskies but equally stubborn. High prey drive.

🏃 Working Purpose

Husky: Built for Speed

  • • Bred to pull light loads FAST
  • • Sled racing dogs
  • • Can run all day at moderate speed
  • • More nimble and agile
  • • Works well in teams

Malamute: Built for Power

  • • Bred to pull heavy loads SLOWLY
  • • Freight hauling dogs
  • • Incredible strength and endurance
  • • More powerful and muscular
  • • Can be more independent

⚠️ Challenges Both Breeds Share

  • 🏃 Escape artists - need secure fencing
  • 🌨️ MASSIVE shedding (twice yearly "coat blow")
  • 🎯 High prey drive - not safe with small pets
  • 🎓 Stubborn - challenging to train
  • ☀️ Struggle in hot climates
  • 🏠 NOT suitable for apartments

🎯 Which Is Right For You?

Choose a Siberian Husky If...

  • ✓ You want those striking blue eyes
  • ✓ You're an active runner/biker
  • ✓ You enjoy a very vocal, "talkative" dog
  • ✓ You want a medium-sized dog
  • ✓ You have multiple dogs (better with others)

Choose an Alaskan Malamute If...

  • ✓ You want a larger, more powerful dog
  • ✓ You prefer slightly calmer energy
  • ✓ You want a more dignified demeanor
  • ✓ You enjoy hiking and weight pulling
  • ✓ You only want one dog (can be dog-aggressive)

The Bottom Line

Both breeds need experienced owners, cold climates, secure yards, and LOTS of exercise. Huskies are smaller, faster, and more social. Malamutes are larger, stronger, and more independent. Neither is for beginners or apartment dwellers. Choose based on size preference and whether you want a "chatterbox" (Husky) or a more quiet dog (Malamute).
